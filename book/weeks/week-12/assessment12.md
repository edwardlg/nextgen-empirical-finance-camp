# Week 12 Assessment — Program-Exit Assessment: Submission Receipts, the Five-Year Memo, and the Exit Interview

Week 11's assessment graded the *manuscript*. Week 12's grades the *submission* — whether the manuscript actually crossed the threshold into the public record, whether you have articulated a coherent five-year research identity, and whether you can defend that articulation in a structured interview. This is the camp's program-exit assessment, weighted **100 points** and the final graded artifact.

The standard, drawn from Mentor 12: **the camp's twelve weeks built the *project-side* of your research identity; today's assessment grades whether you have begun building the *relationship-side*.** Submission receipts, the five-year memo, and the exit-interview transcript are the three lenses through which that discipline becomes visible. Hold yourself to CONVENTIONS §1, §4, §5, and §6.

---

## How you submit

The assessment is one deliverable from three sides: the `submission/receipts/` folder, the five-year memo as `reflections/five-year-research-identity.md`, and the exit-interview transcript filed in your private research journal. Push the public artifacts to your capstone repository's `main` branch; reference the journal-folder transcript filename in your cover note. The grader reviews the public artifacts directly and verifies the journal filename against Prof. Gao's private interview record. Every claim of "I submitted to Schar" is backed by a confirmation screenshot, every "I posted to SSRN" by the live-URL email PDF, every "I deposited in MARS" by the pending or approved librarian email.

## The deliverable spec

### Deliverable 1 — The submission receipts (the verified public record)

**Six files in `submission/receipts/`**, produced as a by-product of Lab 12. The grader checks each for existence, completeness, and consistency with the registry in `submission/handles.md`.

**Schar Young Scholars Journal (30 pts).** Two files: `01_schar_confirmation.png` (the ScholarOne post-submission confirmation page) and `01_schar_email.pdf` (the editorial-office acknowledgment email saved as PDF). The screenshot must show the submission ID of the form `SYSJ-2026-XXXXX`, the date, the manuscript title, the corresponding-author name, and the five uploaded file names. The email PDF must show editorial receipt with the same submission ID. The grader cross-checks the ID against `submission/handles.md`.

**SSRN pre-print posting (20 pts).** Two files: `02_ssrn_queue.png` (the queue-status screenshot taken immediately after clicking *Submit Paper*) and `02_ssrn_live.pdf` (the live-URL email arriving 8-24 hours later). The grader navigates to the live URL and verifies the abstract page is public, network assignment is correct (primary FEN or substantive-fit equivalent, two-to-four secondaries), JEL codes match the manuscript, the abstract is the 245-word version, the Comments-field footnote names the MARS handle (or marks it pending), and Prof. Gao is linked as co-author by ORCID.

**MARS repository deposit (20 pts).** Two files: `03_mars_pending.png` (the post-submission pending-review screenshot) and either `03_mars_approved.pdf` (the librarian-approval email with handle, if it has arrived) or a `03_mars_followup.md` (a 200-word memo documenting the deposit date, temporary deposit ID, and expected handle-arrival date — used when librarian review has not completed by the deadline; full 20 points awarded if the deposit is in the librarian queue and the memo is filed). The grader checks that the metadata matches the manuscript and that the rights statement is CC BY 4.0 rather than the All Rights Reserved default.

### Deliverable 2 — The Five-Year Research-Identity Memo (15 pts)

**A 1,500–2,000-word memo, single-file PDF, committed to the repository as `reflections/five-year-research-identity.pdf`.** The memo follows the Ch 12.5 §10 six-component structure. The grader reads for *honest articulation*, not ambition; an honest "I am most likely going to leave research after college" memo scores full marks if the articulation is specific, well-reasoned, and grounded in the camp's twelve weeks.

The six required sections, each labeled with its heading:

**§1 — Substantive area (~250 words).** Named with specificity. "Banking" is too broad; "the political economy of bank supervision and its effects on credit access in minority and underserved communities" is the right specificity. Name three sub-questions and one ongoing literature debate to engage.

**§2 — Methodological identity (~250 words).** Choose from: causal-inference-on-natural-experiments, structural-estimation, text-and-ML, high-frequency-data, network-analysis, behavioral-experiments, or a defensible hybrid. Name three skills to develop next and the resources you plan to use.

**§3 — Venue identity (~200 words).** Journals, conferences, working-paper series you want your work in. Be honest about tier; "JF eventually" is fine if grounded; "JF as the first submission for the next paper" overclaims unless the next paper is already AEA-credentialed.

**§4 — One-year plan (~250 words).** Specific dated actions in the next twelve months: conferences, revisions, papers to read carefully, senior researchers to reach out to. The most testable component; the grader checks for specific dates.

**§5 — Three-year plan (~250 words).** Structured speculation: second paper, third paper, cumulative direction. Must connect to §1-§2; a "fair lending" §1 paired with a "crypto-microstructure" §5 is a coherence problem.

**§6 — Five-year plan (~300 words).** PhD program, terminal master's, industry research, academic apprenticeship. *Honest about uncertainty* — "I am currently weighing top-ten PhDs vs. consulting careers, deciding on whether the next two years feel sustainable" is *more* mature than false certainty, not less.

### Deliverable 3 — The Structured Exit Interview (15 pts)

**The Lab 12 Part 5 interview transcript, filed in your private research journal as `journal/2026-06-XX-exit-interview.md`.** The transcript is private (not pushed to any public branch); verification is by reference — you name the filename in your cover note, and Prof. Gao confirms the file matches his audio transcript.

The interview is graded on the *quality* of your answers to the twelve structured questions. Strong answers are *specific* (named papers, methods, years), *honest* (acknowledging uncertainty), and *connected* (Q1 headline matches Q2 assumption matches Q3 positioning; Q5 substantive area matches Q7 one-year plan matches Q9 five-year plan). Weak answers are *generic*, *defensive*, or *disconnected*.

### Three rules

1. **Receipts must be real.** A fabricated screenshot is the only integrity violation that automatically zeros the assessment. The grader checks live URLs.
2. **The memo must be in the student's own voice.** A memo reading like Prof. Gao or an AI assistant wrote it is returned for rewriting.
3. **The exit interview is non-redoable.** If it did not happen during Lab 12, the row scores zero, modulo documented emergency.

**Total: 100 points.** The receipt rows together (70 pts) dominate by design — the camp's program-exit requirement is, at base, that you *submitted*. The memo (15) and the interview (15) together (30 pts) are the closing reflective work that turns the submission from an event into the beginning of an arc.

## Failure modes the grader sees, in order of frequency

- **The submission packet that uploaded only the manuscript** (the other four components treated as optional). *Fix:* re-open ScholarOne (editorial office has a 72-hour window to add missing components without restarting).
- **The SSRN posting with the wrong primary network** (FEN chosen for a paper that fits Insurance Law & Policy or Innovation & Patent Law more directly). *Fix:* email SSRN editorial within 48 hours requesting a network change.
- **The MARS deposit defaulted to All Rights Reserved** (clicked through the dropdown without reading). *Fix:* email GMU Libraries within one week requesting CC BY 4.0; librarian can change before public release.
- **The five-year memo that reads as a generic college essay** ("I am passionate about finance" instead of the camp-specific articulation). *Fix:* rewrite §1 first, then rebuild the other five around §1's specificity.
- **The exit interview that did not happen because the student "didn't have time."** *No fix; the row scores zero.*
- **The memo that reads as Prof. Gao's voice** (over-reliance on chapter prose). *Fix:* rewrite the first draft without consulting the chapter.

An Excellent submission shows that the *project-side* discipline of the past twelve weeks has been translated into the *relationship-side* discipline that will carry the work forward. The submission is on the record. The memo names the next decade. The interview commits the relationship between you and the research community to a long horizon. The camp's job is done. The work continues.
